The Ozone Hole
Ozone Hole Size Over Time

In 1984 scientists discovered a hole in the ozone over Antarctica. It was figured out that chlorinated fluorocarbons (CFCs), which are extremely stable in the troposphere, make their way into the stratosphere. There chlorine atoms are split by photons into chlorine radicals, or chlorine atoms with one unpaired electron, which undergo reactions with ozone to make diatomic and monoatomic oxygen. CFCs have been historically used as refrigerants and components of aerosols, due to their inert and cooling properties. International efforts have helped in reducing the amount of CFCs being used, and in 2016 the ozone hole had returned to "moderate" size.
